The Waste Problem

Where $200+ Billion
Disappears Every Year

Enterprise cloud spending has surged past $723 billion, yet an estimated 27-32% of that budget — over $200 billion — is wasted on resources that sit idle, provisioned beyond actual need, or simply forgotten. The waste spans compute, storage, networking, databases, and managed services. Only 6% of organizations report zero avoidable cloud spending.

Multi-Cloud Landscape

The Multi-Cloud Reality

Multi-cloud has become the default enterprise strategy, with 89-92% of enterprises now operating across multiple cloud providers. The average organization uses 3.4 different providers, making unified cost visibility more critical than ever.

AWS maintains market leadership but faces growing pressure from Azure and GCP, both fueled by AI workload demand. Azure grew 39% year-over-year while GCP surged 32%, compared to AWS at 17.5%.

By 2026, over 60% of all enterprise workloads will operate fully in the cloud, with 72% of companies spending more than $1.2 million annually on cloud services alone.
